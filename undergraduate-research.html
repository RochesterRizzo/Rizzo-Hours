Research Project Support

My interests and work are focused on the economics of higher education and various topics in environmental economics. Current or former undergraduates interested in these areas may find projects here worth developing into research assistance, independent research, class projects, presentations, or longer written work.

Higher EducationEnvironmental EconomicsStudent Research

Best fit

These ideas are best for students who can work independently, keep track of details, read carefully, and make steady progress without needing constant supervision.

Economics of Higher Education

  • Net tuition prices, room and board, and the real meaning of college affordability.
  • The sharp decline in community college enrollments.
  • A revisitation of the Bennett Hypothesis.
  • An apportionment analysis of rising college tuition, net pricing, and expenditures.
  • Grade inflation, degree inflation, and labor market outcomes.
  • University administration changes over time and student outcomes.
  • University attendance and the development of critical thinking skills.

Environmental Economics

  • A primer on the expected economic costs of climate change.
  • Real estate values and proximity to renewable-energy projects.
  • The energy transition challenge.
  • Futures contracts, betting markets, and environmental policy.
  • Does it pay for universities to go green?
  • Revisiting Julian Simon-style resource research.
  • War and the environment.
  • LEED design and environmental outcomes.

Birds, Habitat, and Environmental Policy

  • Market mechanisms, habitat banking, biodiversity offsets, and payments for ecosystem services.
  • Property-rights arrangements and governance structures for bird habitats.
  • Polycentric governance and adaptive co-management in avian conservation.
  • Zoning regulations, minimum lot sizes, sprawl, habitat fragmentation, and bird abundance.
  • Birding access, green space, mental-health benefits, and environmental justice.
  • Choice experiments and willingness-to-pay for bird habitat characteristics.
  • Hedonic pricing, urban green infrastructure, bird species richness, and property values.
  • Conservation easements, bird diversity, and spillover effects.

The Fall and Rise of Craft Beer

  • Craft breweries and local economic development, including jobs, tourism, and local business revenues.
  • The environmental footprint of craft beer production.
  • Taxes, licensing, zoning, and the growth of the craft beer industry.
  • Craft breweries, social capital, community cohesion, and civic engagement.

Birds and Brews

  • The economic and environmental interactions between craft breweries and bird conservation efforts.
  • Craft brewery location decisions, bird habitats, and local economic development.
  • Bird-friendly certification, sustainability labels, and market-based conservation incentives.
  • Ecosystem services in the craft beer supply chain.
  • Craft beer and birding events as tools for community engagement and environmental stewardship.

Sports Economics and Other Topics

  • The impact of winning or losing athletic competitions and championships on later life outcomes.
  • The comparative incidence of child abuse in Scouts, churches, schools, and society.
  • Anxiety and mental health as drivers of DI takeup increases.
  • The faculty tax.
  • Win-win government reforms.
  • Gun-control legislation and disparate impact.

What makes a topic promising?

A good undergraduate research topic usually has a clear question, some way to find evidence, and a realistic scope. The best projects are smaller, sharper, and more disciplined than students first imagine.
